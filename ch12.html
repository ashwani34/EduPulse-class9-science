Improvement in Food Resources

Chapter 12 | Biology

Summary

This chapter focuses on the need for and methods of improving crop yields and managing livestock to meet the growing demand for food resources.

‚Ä¢ Crop Improvement

Achieved through hybridization, genetically modified crops, and choosing appropriate varieties for different conditions.

  • Hybridization: Crossing two genetically different plants to create a new, superior variety.
  • Variety Selection: Choosing crops based on yield, quality, and resistance to disease.

‚Ä¢ Nutrient Management

Ensuring soil fertility and adequate supply of essential nutrients (N, P, K, etc.).

  • Manures: Organic matter (e.g., farmyard manure, compost).
  • Fertilizers: Commercial chemicals (e.g., urea, DAP).
[Image of NPK fertilizer bag]

‚Ä¢ Cropping Patterns

  • Mixed Cropping: Growing two or more crops simultaneously in the same field.
  • Inter-cropping: Growing two or more crops in definite row patterns.
  • Crop Rotation: Growing different crops in the same field in a planned succession.

‚Ä¢ Crop Protection

  • Pesticides: Chemicals used to control pests and insects.
  • Weedicides: Chemicals used to control unwanted plants (weeds).
  • Fungicides: Used to control fungal diseases.
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) ‚Äì Combining biological, chemical, and cultural methods.

‚Ä¢ Animal Husbandry

Scientific management of animal livestock (domestic animals) for food and other purposes.

  • Selection of high-yielding breeds of cows, buffaloes, and poultry.
  • Cross-breeding to improve milk, meat, and egg production.
  • Balanced diet, veterinary care, and hygiene improve productivity.
[Image of a dairy farm]

‚Ä¢ Modern Techniques in Food Production

  • Hydroponics ‚Äì Growing plants without soil using nutrient solutions.
  • Sericulture ‚Äì Production of silk from silkworms.
  • Apiculture ‚Äì Bee keeping for honey production.
  • Aquaculture ‚Äì Fish farming in controlled environment.

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • India is the second-largest producer of fruits and vegetables in the world.
  • Hybrid rice and wheat varieties have significantly increased India's food production.
  • Biological pest control (like using ladybugs) is safer than chemical pesticides.
