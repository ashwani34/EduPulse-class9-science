Why Do We Fall Ill?

Chapter 13 | Biology

Summary

This chapter differentiates between being 'healthy' and being 'disease-free,' discusses causes of diseases (infectious and non-infectious), and modes of disease transmission.

‚Ä¢ Health and Disease

  • Health: State of physical, mental, and social well-being.
  • Disease: Disturbance in the structure or function of the body.

‚Ä¢ Types of Diseases

  • Acute: Short duration, severe symptoms (e.g., common cold).
  • Chronic: Long duration, lasting effects (e.g., tuberculosis, diabetes).

‚Ä¢ Causes of Diseases

  • Infectious: Caused by pathogens (e.g., viruses, bacteria).
  • Non-infectious: Internal causes (e.g., genetic disorders, poor diet, lifestyle).

‚Ä¢ Transmission of Diseases

  • Air ‚Äì coughs, sneezes (flu, tuberculosis)
  • Water ‚Äì contaminated water (cholera, typhoid)
  • Vectors ‚Äì mosquitoes, flies (malaria, dengue)
  • Direct contact ‚Äì touching infected person (ringworm)

‚Ä¢ Prevention

  • Vaccination to build immunity
  • Personal hygiene: washing hands, clean food and water
  • Use of mosquito nets, sanitizers
  • Balanced diet and regular exercise

‚Ä¢ Role of Immune System

Protects the body by identifying and destroying pathogens. Produces antibodies to fight infections.

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • Vaccines prevent diseases like polio, measles, and COVID-19.
  • Some bacteria are beneficial for digestion (gut flora).
  • Non-communicable diseases are on the rise due to sedentary lifestyles and unhealthy diet.
