Motion

Chapter 7 | Physics

Summary

This chapter deals with the concept of motion, including speed, velocity, and acceleration. It covers graphical representations of motion and the three equations of motion.

‚Ä¢ Key Concepts

  • Distance: Total path length covered. (Scalar)
  • Displacement: Shortest distance between initial and final points. (Vector)
  • Speed: Distance covered per unit time.
  • Velocity: Displacement per unit time. (Vector)
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity.

‚Ä¢ Equations of Motion (for uniform acceleration)

EquationFormulaRelation
1st Equation$v = u + at$Relation between velocity and time
2nd Equation$s = ut + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$Relation between displacement and time
3rd Equation$v^2 - u^2 = 2as$Relation between velocity and displacement

‚Ä¢ Graphical Representation

  • Distance-time graph: slope = speed
  • Velocity-time graph: slope = acceleration, area under graph = displacement

‚Ä¢ Uniform and Non-Uniform Motion

  • Uniform motion: moves equal distances in equal intervals of time
  • Non-uniform motion: moves unequal distances in equal intervals of time

‚Ä¢ Relative Motion

Motion of an object with respect to another moving object is relative motion. Important in trains, cars, boats, and aeroplanes.

‚Ä¢ Examples from Daily Life

  • Car moving on highway ‚Äì rectilinear motion
  • Earth revolving around sun ‚Äì circular motion
  • Hands of a clock ‚Äì periodic motion
  • Pendulum of a clock ‚Äì oscillatory motion

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • Even standing still, Earth is in motion (rotation + revolution).
  • Motion is relative; two observers may perceive different motion.
  • Graphical method helps to calculate speed, velocity, and acceleration easily.
