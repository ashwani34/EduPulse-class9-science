Force and Laws of Motion

Chapter 8 | Physics

Summary

This chapter introduces the concept of force, the three laws of motion given by Newton, and the concept of momentum and its conservation.

‚Ä¢ Force

A push or a pull that changes or tends to change the state of rest or uniform motion of an object. Force is a vector quantity.

‚Ä¢ Newton‚Äôs First Law (Law of Inertia)

An object remains in a state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

  • Inertia is the tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion or rest.
  • Mass is a measure of inertia.

‚Ä¢ Newton‚Äôs Second Law

The rate of change of momentum is proportional to the applied force, and the change of momentum occurs in the direction of the force.

  • Formula: $F = m a$ (Force = mass $\times$ acceleration)
  • Unit of Force: Newton (N)

‚Ä¢ Newton‚Äôs Third Law

To every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

  • Example: When you jump off a boat, the boat moves backward.
  • Rocket propulsion is a real-life application

‚Ä¢ Momentum

Momentum ($p$) = mass $\times$ velocity

  • Change in momentum = Force $\times$ time
  • Conservation of momentum: In absence of external force, total momentum remains constant

‚Ä¢ Friction

Friction opposes motion. Types:

  • Static friction ‚Äì prevents start of motion
  • Sliding friction ‚Äì opposes motion of sliding objects
  • Rolling friction ‚Äì opposes motion of rolling objects

‚Ä¢ Applications in Daily Life

  • Sports ‚Äì kicks, pushes, throws involve Newton‚Äôs laws
  • Walking ‚Äì friction helps in movement
  • Rocket launch ‚Äì action-reaction

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • Inertia is why heavy objects are harder to start moving.
  • Newton‚Äôs third law is why a gun recoils when fired.
