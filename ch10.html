Work and Energy

Chapter 10 | Physics

Summary

This chapter defines work, different forms of energy (Kinetic and Potential), and power. It also introduces the fundamental Law of Conservation of Energy.

‚Ä¢ Work Done

Work is done when a force causes displacement of an object in the direction of the force.

  • Formula: Work ($W$) = Force ($F$) $\times$ Displacement ($s$)
  • Unit of Work: Joule (J)
  • Work is zero if force and displacement are perpendicular.
[Image illustrating work done by a constant force]

‚Ä¢ Kinetic Energy (KE)

Energy possessed by an object due to its motion.

  • Formula: $KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2$

‚Ä¢ Potential Energy (PE)

Energy stored in an object due to its position or shape.

  • Gravitational PE: $PE = m g h$ (mass $\times$ gravity $\times$ height)
[Image of a roller coaster illustrating KE and PE conversion]

‚Ä¢ Power

Rate of doing work or the rate of transfer of energy.

  • Formula: Power ($P$) = $\frac{\text{Work}}{\text{Time}}$
  • Unit of power: Watt (1 Watt = 1 Joule/second)

‚Ä¢ Forms of Energy

  • Mechanical energy = KE + PE
  • Chemical energy ‚Äì stored in fuels and food
  • Electrical energy ‚Äì from electric current
  • Solar, thermal, nuclear energy

‚Ä¢ Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy can neither be created nor destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to another.

  • Example: Pendulum ‚Äì PE converts to KE and back
  • Example: Solar panel converts solar energy to electrical energy

‚Ä¢ Examples from Daily Life

  • Riding a bicycle ‚Äì converts chemical energy to mechanical energy
  • Lifting an object ‚Äì chemical energy from muscles converted to PE
  • Electric fan ‚Äì electrical energy converted to mechanical energy

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • Work done is zero if there is no displacement, even if force is applied.
  • Kinetic energy depends on the square of velocity, so doubling speed quadruples KE.
  • Energy cannot be destroyed; it only changes form ‚Äì energy is all around us!
