Is Matter Around Us Pure?

Pure vs Impure

In science, "pure" doesn't just mean clean. It means the substance consists of only one type of particle.

1. Classification of Matter

Pure Substances

Consistent chemical properties throughout.

  • Elements: Cannot be broken down (e.g., Gold, Oxygen).
  • Compounds: Fixed ratio of elements (e.g., Water H₂O, Salt NaCl).

Mixtures

Contains more than one substance mixed physically.

  • Homogeneous: Uniform composition (e.g., Sugar in water).
  • Heterogeneous: Distinct parts (e.g., Sand in water, Oil & water).

2. Solutions & Concentration

A solution is a homogeneous mixture of two or more substances. It consists of a Solute (smaller amount) and a Solvent (larger amount).

Saturated Solution

No more solute can be dissolved at a given temperature.

Solubility

Maximum amount of solute that can dissolve in 100g of solvent.

Concentration Formula (Mass %)
$\frac{\text{Mass of Solute}}{\text{Mass of Solution}} \times 100$

*Remember: Mass of Solution = Mass of Solute + Mass of Solvent

3. Suspensions vs Colloids

Property Solution Colloid Suspension
Size < 1 nm 1 nm - 1000 nm > 1000 nm
Tyndall Effect No Yes (Scatters Light) Yes
Stability Very Stable Stable Unstable (settles)

The Tyndall Effect: When a beam of light becomes visible as it passes through a colloid (like sunlight through dust or fog).

4. Separation of Mixtures

Evaporation

Separating a non-volatile solute from a volatile solvent (e.g., Salt from Water).

Centrifugation

Separating dense particles from lighter ones by spinning (e.g., Cream from Milk).

Chromatography

Separating solutes that dissolve in the same solvent (e.g., Dyes in Black Ink).

Distillation

Separating miscible liquids with different boiling points (>25K difference).

Physical Change

No new substance is formed. Reversible. Changes in state, color, or shape.

Example: Melting ice, Tearing paper.

Chemical Change

New substance is formed with different properties. Irreversible.

Example: Burning wood, Rusting iron, Digestion.
