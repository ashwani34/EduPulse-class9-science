Summary

This chapter explores the organization of cells into tissues in both plants and animals. It covers the types, functions, and locations of different tissue systems.

‚Ä¢ Plant Tissues

1. Meristematic Tissue

Responsible for growth and cell division. Found in growing regions (apical, lateral, intercalary).

[Image of the types of Meristematic Tissues]

2. Permanent Tissue

Formed from meristematic tissue after cell differentiation.

  • Simple: Parenchyma, Collenchyma, Sclerenchyma.
  • Complex: Xylem (water transport), Phloem (food transport).

‚Ä¢ Animal Tissues

[Image of the four types of Animal Tissues]

1. Epithelial Tissue

Covering or protective tissues. Found on the outer skin, lining of mouth, blood vessels.

2. Connective Tissue

Connects different parts of the body. Includes blood, bone, cartilage, ligament, tendon, areolar tissue, adipose tissue.

  • Blood is a fluid connective tissue.
  • Bone is a solid connective tissue.

3. Muscular Tissue

Responsible for movement.

  • Striated (skeletal) ‚Äì voluntary movement
  • Smooth ‚Äì involuntary, walls of organs
  • Cardiac ‚Äì heart muscles, involuntary

4. Nervous Tissue

Transmits electrical impulses and coordinates body activities.

  • Neurons ‚Äì functional unit
  • Found in brain, spinal cord, nerves
