Gravitation

Chapter 9 | Physics

Summary

This chapter explains the Universal Law of Gravitation, the concept of acceleration due to gravity ($g$), mass versus weight, and the application of these principles to free fall and orbital motion.

‚Ä¢ Universal Law of Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

  • Formula: $F = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$
  • $G$ is the Universal Gravitational Constant.

‚Ä¢ Acceleration due to Gravity ($g$)

  • The acceleration produced by the Earth's gravitational pull.
  • Value on Earth: $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
  • Formula: $g = \frac{G M}{R^2}$

‚Ä¢ Free Fall

An object falling under the influence of gravity alone is in free fall.

  • Initial velocity $u = 0$ if dropped from rest
  • Displacement: $s = \frac{1}{2} g t^2$
  • Velocity after time $t$: $v = g t$

‚Ä¢ Orbital Motion

Objects move in circular orbits under the influence of centripetal force provided by gravity.

  • Planets revolve around the Sun due to Sun‚Äôs gravitational pull
  • Artificial satellites remain in orbit due to Earth‚Äôs gravity and their tangential velocity

‚Ä¢ Important Points

  • Force of gravitation keeps planets, moons, and satellites in motion
  • Gravity decreases with height above Earth's surface
  • Weightlessness occurs when gravitational pull is balanced by free fall motion (e.g., in space)

‚Ä¢ Fun Facts

  • Moon‚Äôs gravity is $1/6$th of Earth‚Äôs gravity.
  • All objects fall at the same rate ignoring air resistance.
  • Gravitational force is responsible for tides on Earth.
